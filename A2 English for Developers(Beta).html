Greetings in your first day at the office

In this first course, you'll learn common expressions for situations you may encounter on your first day at work.
You'll learn about introductions, getting to know people, asking for lunch recommendations, and getting an access card from security.

Dialogue 1: Maria Introduces Herself to Tom

Hello. You're the new graphic designer, right?

  1. Contractions are commonly used to make speech sound more natural and fluent. You're is a contraction of you are.
    Are is used here in the present affirmative to make a statement or ask a question.
    This is a typical way to confirm someone's role or identity in English.
    Contractions are a way to shorten common combinations of words, especially with verbs.
  2. To check or confirm something people sometimes use tag questions. For example, You're a programmer, right?
    Here, right? is used as a tag to check or confirm the previous statement.

I'm Maria, the team lead.

  1. We often mention our job or role in a company by saying, I'm [name], the [job title].
    Name refers to the person's name, and job title is their occupation. This lets others know our position or role.

Hi, that's right! I'm Tom McKenzie. It's a pleasure to meet you.

  1. When someone makes a correct assumption or guess about you, you can confirm it using phrases like that's right.
    It's a way of agreeing or saying yes to what is said.
  2. The verb to be is used to talk about identities, characteristics, and more.
  3. Expressions like It's a pleasure to meet you are commonly used when meeting someone for the first time as a polite way to greet someone.

Welcome aboard, Tom! How do you like California so far?

  1. The expression Welcome aboard is used to greet someone who has just joined a group, team, or organization. It's a warm way of saying "We're glad you're here with us now".
  2. When you want to ask someone for their opinion or feelings about something, especially a place or an experience, you can use the phrase How do you like.
  3. Maria is asking Tom about his impressions of California.
  4. The phrase so far is used in English to indicate the time up to the present moment or up to a certain point.

I like it. It's different from Texas, but I like it here.

  1. When talking about preferences or feelings towards something, the phrase I like it is often used.
  2. When you want to highlight differences between two things, you can use the phrase different from.
    It helps to describe how one thing is not the same as another.
  3. When talking about places or things, especially when comparing or describing them, it's serves as a subject and verb starter for the sentence.
  4. When expressing a positive feeling about a place, one might use the phrase I like it here.
    The phrase I like it here is commonly used to express positive feelings about a current location or environment.
    When someone says this, they are often commenting on their general comfort, happiness, or satisfaction with the place they are in.
  5. Tom is emphasizing his contentment in California, despite it being different from Texas.

Let me show you to your desk.

  1. When someone wants to guide or direct another person somewhere, they can use let me show you to your [place], place being the destination.
    The phrase let me show you is an offer of assistance or guidance. It expresses the speaker's intention to demonstrate or direct something to the listener.
  2. A desk is often the central part of one's workspace, especially in an office setting.
    People typically use it to set up their computers, write, read, and do other work-related tasks.

Do you see the desk with a drawing tablet and a computer? That is your workspace.

  1. A drawing tablet is a tool that people use with computers to draw or design things.
  2. Describing objects or places with their characteristics or things related to them helps the listener to identify them easily.
  3. The term workspace is often used to describe the area where someone works, especially in an office environment.

Everything looks great, thanks for showing me around the place, Meria.

  1. Expressing satisfaction or contentment in English can be done using various expressions.
    Everything looks great is one way to show that you are pleased or satisfied with something.
  2. Expressing gratitude is common in the workplace, especially when someone helps you.
    Tom thanks Maria for something specific.
  3. When expressing gratitude, we sometimes mention the action for which we are thankful. Here, Tom is specifically thanking Maria for something she did.
